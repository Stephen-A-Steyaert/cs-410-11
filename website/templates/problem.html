The Problem

Why students struggle to translate coursework into workforce readiness, and where the current process breaks.

Problem Statement

Universities clearly define academic degree requirements, yet professional development opportunities — including internships, certifications, networking events, alumni engagement, and local experiences — exist across separate platforms and resources. Students must independently connect their coursework to evolving market expectations without a unified, data-informed system to identify skill gaps or find relevant opportunities in real time. This structural disconnect leads to reactive decision-making and delayed workforce readiness.

Problem Characteristics

  • Fragmented Academic & Opportunity Systems

    Students must navigate DegreeWorks, Canvas, Handshake, LinkedIn, clubs, certifications, and advisors separately. No unified system connects academic progress to local professional opportunities.

  • Reactive Career Engagement Model

    Universities provide optional career resources that are passive and depend on student initiative. Opportunities are time-sensitive but not dynamically matched to student progress.

  • Lack of Structured Skill Alignment

    There is no structured comparison between academic progress, local employer expectations, and industry benchmarks. Students lack clear visibility into missing credentials and role readiness.
